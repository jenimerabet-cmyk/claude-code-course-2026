Week 4 Reflection

Where I stand on AI:

Now that I’ve seen how AI actually works under the hood, I don’t really see it as magic anymore. It’s more like a tool that follows patterns and uses tools in a system, not something that just thinks on its own. I think I can use AI ethically as long as I’m not relying on it to do everything for me. For me, it makes sense to use it for things like understanding code, fixing mistakes, or explaining concepts, but not for just copying answers without learning anything. In school projects, I would use AI as support when I’m stuck or need help understanding something, especially in studying. But I would still make sure I actually understand what it gives me and not just paste answers blindly.

One thing that clicked:

One thing that finally made sense for me was the whole tool use cycle. I used to think Claude just “answers” questions directly, but now I understand it actually goes through a process where it decides to use tools, sends a tool_use request, and then waits for the result before responding. The messages array also clicked for me because it shows how everything is stored step by step. It’s basically how the model keeps track of what happened before it gives the final response.
