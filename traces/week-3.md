Week 3 Prompt Trace

Original Prompt:
Explain the water cycle for my Bio Class.

Original Response:
The water cycle is the process where water moves through evaporation, condensation, and precipitation. It is important for maintaining Earth’s water supply.

What was wrong with it
It was too short and kinda basic. It didn’t really help me understand it properly because it just listed the steps without explaining them clearly.

Rewritten Prompt:
Explain the water cycle for a Grade 11 science test. Break it into clear steps like evaporation, condensation, precipitation, collection and explain each one in simple terms with examples so I can actually study it.

New Response:
The water cycle is how water keeps moving around Earth.

1. Evaporation: The sun heats up water in oceans, lakes, and rivers and turns it into water vapour that goes into the air. For example, puddles disappearing after a sunny day.

2. Condensation: The water vapour cools down in the sky and turns into clouds. This is why you see clouds forming.

3. Precipitation: When clouds get too heavy, the water falls back down as rain, snow, or hail.

4. Collection: The water collects again in rivers, lakes, and oceans and the cycle repeats.


What changed and why it worked:
I made the prompt more specific by adding my grade level and asking for simple explanations with examples. That stopped the answer from being too vague and made it easier to study from.
